What are gemstones?

Gemstones are minerals that are extracted from the mountains. These extracted minerals are then sold in the form of rough, cut and specimen gemstones. Not all minerals are suitable for gemstones. With beauty, a mineral must possess hardness and stability, and occur in crystals or masses large enough for cutting. Such material is very rare. Many minerals are small, opaque or easily damaged. Out of over 4000 known minerals, only about 100 minerals are ever worked and only about 20 are commonly used in jewellery. A number of opaque minerals and rocks with attractive colours and patterns are polished to make beads, carvings, bowls and decorative items. Such minerals are broadly termed 'ornamentals'.

Rough Gemstones

Gemstones in their natural form are known as rough gemstones. Among these rough gemstones, cut gemstones can be obtained. Rough gemstones are comparatively cheaper than the cut gemstones because of the fact that they are in the raw form.

Specimen Stones

The gemstone specimen are the minerals that cannot fashioned into cut gemstones, rather they look beautiful in their natural form. Quartz, Tourmaline, Ruby and Sapphire are widely used as the gemstone specimens.

Cut Stones
